Pro Tips

Make These Certs Actually Work For You

On Your Resume

Add Every Certificate to LinkedIn

Under "Licenses & Certifications" — add each one with the issuing organization (Microsoft, Cisco, Google, freeCodeCamp, Microsoft) and 2026 as the year. Recruiters actively filter by certifications. These 5 will make your profile appear in searches.

On Your Resume

List the Skills, Not Just the Courses

Don't write "Completed Excel course." Write: "Excel — VLOOKUP, Pivot Tables, data cleaning (Great Learning × Microsoft)." Be specific about what you can do. Hiring managers scan for tool names, not course names.

For Interviews

Use the Projects as Portfolio Proof

The Cisco course gives you a movies dataset project. The freeCodeCamp course gives you 5 Python projects. Put them on GitHub. Link to them in your resume. When an interviewer asks "show me something you've built" — you have an answer.

The Stack You'll Have

Excel → SQL → Python → Power BI → AI

After completing all 5 in order, your resume covers the full 2026 data analyst stack. That is: Excel, SQL, Python, Pandas, NumPy, Tableau, Power BI, and AI tools. Most entry-level job descriptions require 3-4 of these. You'll have all of them.
