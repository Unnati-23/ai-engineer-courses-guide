The Playbook

China Did This Before. In 2000, It Was Steel.

China flooded the steel market with product priced 50% below competitors. America's biggest mills shut down. Obama imposed 236% anti-dumping duties. Too late — China was already producing half the world's steel. The US? Just 7%.

Now it's running the exact same playbook with AI. Everything the US spent billions building, China either gave away for free — or priced 90% cheaper.

1

Flood with Open Source

DeepSeek, Qwen, GLM — released freely while US labs charge premium prices.

2

Price 90% Below Competitors

Chinese API pricing undercuts OpenAI and Anthropic by 80–90% on equivalent tasks.

3

Scale Adoption, Not Just Models

China installed 295,000 industrial robots last year — nearly 9x the US (34,200). AI adoption in manufacturing is already dominant.

4

Energy Advantage

China's electricity reserve margin has never dipped below 80% — twice necessary capacity. The US power grid is identified as a key AI bottleneck in the Stanford report.

The Silent Crisis

AI Talent Migration to the US Has Dropped 89%

Since 2017, the number of AI researchers moving to the US has dropped 89% — with 80% of that decline happening in just the last year. The Stanford report calls it "precipitous." Switzerland now ranks #1 globally for AI researchers per capita.

What this means: The US spending advantage buys hardware and infrastructure — but not the intellectual capital that turns compute into capability. DeepSeek proved in January 2025 that a Chinese lab could match Silicon Valley's best with a fraction of the resources. The conditions producing that are strengthening, not weakening.

What This Means For You

The Real Takeaway from the Stanford Report

Bottom Line
  • The US leads on investment and top model performance — but by less than 3%.
  • China leads on patents (69.7% of global filings), publications, robotics, and energy infrastructure.
  • The performance gap has collapsed from 31.6% to 2.7% in 3 years — while the spending gap grew to 23x.
  • AI talent migration to the US dropped 89% — the US advantage is hardware, not people.
  • Chinese models are free or 90% cheaper — and nearly as good. That changes everything for builders.
  • The best strategy in 2026: use the right model for the right job. Don't be loyal to one ecosystem.
