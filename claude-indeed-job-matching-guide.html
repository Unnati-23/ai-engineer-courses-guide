Turn Claude into your personal job-matching recruiter

Upload your resume, connect two tools, paste one prompt — and get a colour-coded, honest set of fresh roles posted in the last 48 hours, each with a callback-likelihood score, salary, an Apply button, and a one-click "tailor my resume" button.

A free guide by Unnati Tripathi ↗ INSTAGRAM
Step 1

Connect the Indeed connector

  1. 1In Claude, click "Search and tools" near the message box (or go to Settings → Connectors).
  2. 2Select "Add connectors" and search for Indeed.
  3. 3Click the Indeed card, then click "Connect" and follow the authentication prompt.
  4. 4Once it shows as connected, move to Step 3 below and try the prompt.
Heads up

This is the officially supported route, and Anthropic's own docs say it should let Claude search and match jobs directly. In practice it doesn't always expose live search the same way for every account yet. If Claude says it can't actually search Indeed from this connector, that's your cue to lean on Step 2 instead — don't let it talk you into faking results from memory.

Step 2

Give Claude access to Chrome

  1. 1Go to the Chrome Web Store and search "Claude for Chrome", or visit claude.ai/chrome. Click "Add to Chrome."
  2. 2Sign in with your Claude account when prompted, then pin the extension: click the puzzle-piece icon in your toolbar and tap the pin next to Claude.
  3. 3In Claude's Settings → Connectors, find "Claude in Chrome" and toggle it on.
  4. 4Back in your chat, when Claude needs the browser it will ask which connected browser to use — pick yours and confirm. That's your only manual step; Claude drives the rest, opening Indeed and reading listings itself.
Worth knowing

Claude in Chrome needs a paid plan (Pro, Max, Team, or Enterprise) and only works in actual Google Chrome, not other Chromium browsers or mobile. If you don't see it yet, it's still rolling out — check claude.ai/chrome for access.

It browses as you, in your real signed-in browser — that's exactly why it works where plain connectors sometimes don't. Keep it to reading job listings; don't ask it to apply, message, or change anything in your account.

Step 3

Upload your resume + paste this prompt

Upload your resume as a PDF or image first. Edit the four highlighted lines below to match what you actually want, then paste the whole thing into the same chat. The freshness window is already locked to the last 48 hours.

I want you to act as a job-matching recruiter and find me real, currently open roles based on my resume.

Step 1 — Analyse my resume: skills, experience, seniority, industry fit, tools/software, education, and transferable skills.

Step 2 — Use your Indeed connector, or your connected Chrome browser if the connector isn't searching live, to actually navigate to Indeed and search. Don't rely on general web search results alone — open each individual listing and read the full description before scoring it. Only include listings posted within the last 48 hours — check each listing's "posted X hours/days ago" label and use Indeed's date-posted filter to narrow this down.

My preferences (edit these before sending):
- Target roles / industries: [e.g. Finance, Analyst, Strategy, Consulting — replace with yours]
- Location(s): [e.g. Bengaluru, Mumbai, Remote — replace with yours]
- Salary expectation: [e.g. ₹15–25L per year, or "no preference"]
- Remote/hybrid: [Remote only / Hybrid OK / Open to in-office]

Step 3 — Search thoroughly enough to find genuine strong matches, not just moderate ones. Specifically run searches using my most distinctive, exact skills and named tools as keywords — the ones a generic candidate in this field wouldn't have. Those produce the strongest real fits. Try multiple search angles before concluding. If, after a thorough search, there's genuinely only one strong match (or none) within the last 48 hours, tell me that honestly instead of inflating a moderate match into a strong one just to hit a number.

Step 4 — For every role, score the likelihood of getting a callback/interview based on resume fit (not a guess at final hiring odds — just how likely my resume clears the first screen):
🟢 70–100% = Strong match
🟡 40–69% = Moderate match
🔴 Below 40% = Weak match
Base this on real skill overlap, years of experience, ATS keyword match, and industry alignment. Don't inflate scores to be encouraging.

Step 5 — Show every real, currently open role posted in the last 48 hours that you actually opened and read, not just the strong ones. Rank everything highest to lowest.

Step 6 — Present this as a colourful interactive visual, one card per role, colour-coded by tier (green/amber/red), showing: Job Title, Company, Location, estimated Salary, the callback-likelihood score, Why It Matches, and Missing Skills. Next to each role, give me two buttons side by side: an Apply button linking directly to the real listing, and a "Tailor my resume for this role" button. When I click it (or just tell you which role), generate an actual downloadable file with my resume rewritten for that exact job — summary, bullets, and keywords — so I can download it and then click Apply.

Step 7 — After the cards, add: Top 3 Highest Probability Roles, Fastest Path to Increase Hiring Odds, Skills Hurting My Application Most, Resume Keywords I'm Missing, and Best Companies to Realistically Target Right Now.

Be brutally honest throughout. If my resume doesn't genuinely fit one of my target categories, say so directly instead of forcing in a weak match. Flag clearly any listing where you could only get snippet-level detail instead of the full job description.

Customize
Change the four bracketed lines for any role, city, or salary band. The structure doesn't change, only your inputs do.
Verify
Treat the cards as a shortlist, not a final answer. Open the apply link yourself before you act on anything Claude tells you.
Re-run
Since this only searches the last 48 hours, re-run the same prompt every couple of days to catch fresh postings as they go live.
